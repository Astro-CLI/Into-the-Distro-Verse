Linux & Open Source History

The history of Linux and the open-source movement is a fascinating journey of collaboration, innovation, and the pursuit of digital freedom.

Linus Torvalds

Linus Torvalds is the creator and principal developer of the Linux kernel. In 1991, as a student at the University of Helsinki, he started developing a free operating system kernel as a hobby.

Richard Stallman

Richard Stallman is the founder of the Free Software movement and the GNU Project. He launched the GNU Project in 1983 with the goal of creating a completely free operating system.

Linux (The Operating System)

While often referred to simply as "Linux," the operating system most people use is actually a combination of the Linux kernel and various GNU tools and libraries, often called GNU/Linux.

GNU Project

The GNU Project was launched by Richard Stallman in 1983 to develop a free Unix-like operating system. Many of the tools used in Linux distributions today come from the GNU Project.

Git

Git is a distributed version control system created by Linus Torvalds in 2005 for the development of the Linux kernel. It has since become the most widely used version control system in the world.

FOSS (Free and Open Source Software)

FOSS refers to software that is both free (as in freedom) and open-source. It allows anyone to view, modify, and distribute the source code.

Free Software Foundation

The Free Software Foundation (FSF) is a non-profit organization founded by Richard Stallman in 1985 to support the free software movement.

Open Source Initiative

The Open Source Initiative (OSI) is a non-profit organization dedicated to promoting open-source software.

Linux Mascot History

Tux the Penguin

Tux is the official mascot of the Linux kernel. Created by Larry Ewing in 1996, Tux has become a globally recognized symbol for Linux.

The penguin was chosen as the Linux mascot during a contest, and his image perfectly embodied the free and open nature of Linux. Tux's friendly and approachable design made Linux feel less intimidating to newcomers, helping drive adoption of the operating system. Since then, Tux has appeared on countless distributions, promotional materials, and community projects.

Xenia the Fox

Xenia is a community-created mascot, often associated with the Linux movement, particularly within certain circles of the Linux community.

The fox character embodies a different aesthetic while maintaining the same values of openness and community. Xenia appeals to a younger generation and the creative communities that have embraced Linux and free software. Her vibrant personality and artistic designs have made her popular in memes, fan art, and community discussions across social media and forums.

Both Tux and Xenia represent the diverse and inclusive nature of the Linux and open-source communities.
